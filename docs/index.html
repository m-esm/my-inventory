From a part in your hand to a catalog entry

Three steps, and the only thing you type is the bag it goes in.

Step 1 · Scan

Hold the part up to your phone

Open the capture page and point any camera at the component. A phone over macOS Continuity Camera works great. Snap the part, the label, the back of the board: as many shots as you want for one item.

Step 2 · Claude identifies

Claude reads the photo and names it

This is the part you don't do. Claude looks at your shots and writes back the name, category, condition and the specs and SKU printed on the label. It catches duplicates, orders the part photo first, and flags anything it isn't sure about so you can confirm it.

Step 3 · Find & share

Search it, share it, restock it

Filter by category, location or free text and read the bag map at a glance. Send a collaborator a read-only link to the whole catalog. Keep a "to buy" list for what you're out of, with a public endpoint so an agent can request a part with no login.

What you get

Built around recognizing the part, not around a spreadsheet you have to feed.

🧠

Claude identifies the part

Name, category, condition, specs and SKU read straight off the photo. The image is the source of truth; the metadata writes itself.

📸

Scan with your phone

Multiple shots per item from Continuity Camera or any webcam. The photo that shows the part becomes the card thumbnail automatically.

🏷️

Reads the label too

Point it at the back of the bag and Claude lifts the SKU, pack count and specs, then merges them into the entry it already made.

🗃️

Duplicate-aware

Re-scan a part you own and it surfaces a card to top up, set, or split the stash by bag. No silent double entries.

🔓

View public, edit private

Share a read-only link with anyone. Editing is gated behind a login. Manage users from the dashboard.

🛒

"To buy" list for agents

An MCP server lets an agent search your stock and request a part it needs with no credentials. Mark it bought when it arrives.

Open source, and entirely yours

my-inventory is a small, self-hosted app with no database and no build step. Your photos and data stay on your machine or your own server. Run it locally in one command, or put it behind Docker or Caddy to share.

Bring your own Claude setup to do the identifying, or skip it and type names yourself. Either way the inventory is yours.
